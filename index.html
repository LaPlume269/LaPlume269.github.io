Recipe #1: Pilao

  • Time: 50 min
  • Cooking: 45 min
Ingredients Quantity
Chicken 1
Onions 2
Rice 500g
Turmeric 1 tbsp
Cinnamon sticks 2
Cloves 3
Tomatoes 3
Tomato concentrate 2 tbsp
Olive oil 4 tbsp
Large cloves of garlic 2
Bouillon cube 1
Piment
Salt and pepper

Step 1:

Cut the chicken into 8 pieces. Peel and slice the onions.

Step 2 :

Fry the onions in hot oil in a large pot. When they begin to brown, add the chicken pieces. Brown everything well.

Step 3 :

Add the tomato paste, spices and minced garlic. Mix quickly.

Step 4 :

Cut the tomatoes into small pieces and add them to the chicken. Leave to cook for 20 minutes.

Cooking is finished when the rice is cooked.

Recipe #2: Mkatra Siniya

  • Time: 10 min
  • Cooking: 20 min
Ingredients Quantity
Surinam rice 1 kg
Coconut 2
Cardamom seed 1 tsp
Sodium bicarbonate 1 tsp
Baking powder 1 half bag
Coconut milk 75 ml
Powdered sugar 450 g
Vanilla bean 1

Step 1/Mkatra Siniya:

Soak the rice in a large container for 3 days in a room at room temperature. Change the soaking water every evening.

Step 2/Mkatra Siniya:

On the fourth day, rinse the rice well and drain for 30 minutes.

Step 3/Mkatra Siniya:

Preheat the oven at 180Â°C.

Step 4/Mkatra Siniya:

In a blender, grind the rice, bicarbonate of soda and coconut milk until liquid.

Step 5/Mkatra Siniya:

Add cardamom and vanilla seeds. Blend again.

Step 6/Mkatra Siniya:

Add all the remaining ingredients and blend again.

Step 7/Mkatra Siniya:

Line a baking pan with this preparation and place in the oven for 20 to 30 minutes.

Step 8/MkatraSiniya:

Insert the blade of a knife after 20 minutes (not before) to check the state of baking inside the cake.

Step 9/Mkatra Siniya:

If the cake is golden but not baked inside, cover it with a baking sheet to the end.

Recipe #3: Mkatra foutra
  • Time: 40 min
  • Cooking: 20 min
Ingredients Quantity
Flour 1 kg
Coconut milk 1 L
Salt
Baker's yeat
Egg 2
Beef meat
Tomato concentrate 2 boxes
Tomatoes 2
Onion 1
Wing pods 2
Oil 1 L
Parsley
Cumin
Pepper
Turmeric

Step 1/Mkatra Foutra: For the dough

Pour 1 kg of flour into a salad bowl and make a well.

Step 2/Mkatra Foutra:

Pour the yeast into a glass half-filled with water.

Step 3/Mkatra Foutra:

Beat the eggs and add them to the flour and coconut milk.

Step 4/Mkatra Foutra:

Add a teaspoon of salt and mix the yeast with the hot water and add it to the preparation.

Step 5/Mkatra Foutra:

Mix everything with a mixer or whisk until you obtain a smooth, thick paste (not too liquid) and leave to rest.

Step 6/Mkatra Foutra: For the sauce

Cut the meat into small pieces and set aside.

Step 7/Mkatra Foutra:

Mince the onion and garlic, then chop the tomatoes.

Step 8/Mkatra Foutra:

Put the pot on the heat, add 2 tbsp of oil. Leave to heat then add the onions and garlic.

Step 9/Mkatra Foutra:

Mix and let melt, then add 2 pinches of cumin and a pinch of pepper. Mix and add the tomatoes.

Step 10/Mkatra Foutra:

Mix and add the 2 cans of tomato paste. Mix again.

Step 11/Mkatra Foutra:

Add the meat (cook the meat in another pot before making the sauce with it) and a teaspoon of salt and a pinch of parsley. Mix again and cover.

Leave to cook for 15 minutes.

Put the pan on low heat without buttering it. Leave without fat then pour a ladle of preparation. Cover and watch that there is no more liquid paste.

Turn over and leave it for a few seconds, then remove it and put it on a dry plate and so on until there is no more dough.
